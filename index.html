Music For the Moving Image Compositions

Assignment 1 - Poem 3

The basis of this composition is drone music, best listened to through headphones or speakers. I have looked to artists that are considered the pioneers of minimalism, those such as Steve Reich, Phillip Glass, La Monte Young and Arvo PÃ¤rt. Drone music is considered a sub-genre of minimalism and unlike traditional minimalist music such as the works of Phillip Glass (which uses sustain and prolongation in the form of many repeated notes). Drone music emphasizes the use of single sustained notes either in polyphonic textures or monphonic on occasion i.e tone clusters. I was also fascinated by the effect I knew slight differentiation in tone would have on the physical world, not just sonically, but its actual effect on the human body. A concept I discovered in my lectures with the Ensemble For The Moving Image led by William Crosby. I learnt that when notes sustained are a semitone apart, the dissonance seems to create a sort of pulse in the air that can be affected by interacting with it as a physical prescence. I wanted to recreated this effect with synthesisers and have resulted in this piece. The effects relation to the poem bears great significance, the way the music sounds when listened to in a binaural space (headphones, or multiple placed speakers), makes the listener feel as though they are encompassed by the sound, much as they would be encompassed by water or earth or the emptiness of space. All of which are heavily implied or outrightly stated in the poem. There are subtle samples of screams in this piece, lowered in pitch to match the pulse, but is a reference to the line "Gaia's broken throat".
